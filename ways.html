How to reduce your Carbon footprint.

Home energy reduction techniques

  • Turn up thermostat in summer and turn down heating in winter.
    • Even modest changes can save over $30.00 a year and cut your CO2 emission by over 400 lbs!
  • Replace incandescent lightbulbs with energy star lights.
    • Can save $20.00 a year and 150 lbs of CO2.
  • Use cold water in your washer machine rather than hot water, and hang dry rather than using the dryer.
    • Can save $100.00 a year and 700 lbs of CO2.
  • Replace your refridgerator with a newer, energy efficient model.
    • Can save $100.00 a year and 700 lbs of CO2.
  • Replace Windows with new, more energy efficient panes.
    • Can save more than $150.00, and 2,947 lbs of CO2 a year.

Transportation

  • Consider taking an alternate form of Transportation!
    • The Max lightrail and the bus produces 2-5 times less lbs of CO2 compared to cars!
    • Conversely, walking and biking produces zero lbs of CO2!
  • Regular maintenance on vehicles can save $214 and 1,157 lbs of CO2 a year!.
  • Reduce driving miles! Consider walking to the store or show, taking out drive miles that aren't necessary. Savings are proportional to your mpg and amount of mile reduction.
  • Carpool to work or school! It's an easy way to cut car emissions down by approximately half.

Waste

  • By recycling metals, plastics and papers, you can cut CO2 emissions by 291 lbs a year!

Food

  • Consider eating less meat! Food processing involved with a household's consumption is estimated to be the third largest conributor to CO2 emission! Lamb, beef and highly processed cheese are among the largest contributors to food-process based CO2 emissions. Consider sticking to more CO2 efficient foods like:
    • Vegetables
    • Eggs
    • Milk
    • Lentils
    • Beans
    • Nuts
    • Rice
    • Potatoes
