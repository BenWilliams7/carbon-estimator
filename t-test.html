Compare results between cohorts

A t-test to help help see differences between results.

Our t-test can analyze emission results between two cohorts at a time to see if there is a "statistically valid" difference between groups (we say "statistically valid" in quotations because our emission calculator is imperfect and subjective, rather than observational and objective, so these results should be taken with a grain of salt, but are still useful in relative terms).

Using data methods like sample size, average, and standard deviation, we can produce degrees of freedom and a t-result. To get an exact p-value would require some fancy integral calculus that we just don't have the time for, but by using our results, and setting an alpha of 0.05, we can ascertain whether or not two data-sets are different with a 95% confidence interval. That's a pretty high bar for most! Our null hypothesis is that the two averages are similar (data is within 95% normal distribution). If the null hypothesis is rejected, this means the two cohorts are statistically different! One big assumption to be made with t-tests are that the variances are equal (roughly equated to standard deviations).
